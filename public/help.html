Information help

Suggested Sections

At the minimum, in the Information portion of your syllabus provide:

Syllabus Title

The course title, the course number, and the current term designation.

Contact Information (Section 1)

Your title and primary contact method. Provide information about your communication preferences --when you will check email, how quickly you will respond, etc. --in the Course Policies section.

View Policies help

If you are team teaching and/or have a Teaching Assistant, use the ”add contact” tool to add up to three additional Contacts.

Course Description (Section 3)

A description of your course. Here are some suggested formats:
  • Objective-based narrative: link together your Objectives to tell the ”story” of your course
  • Questions: pose a series of questions that the course will help your student answer
  • Anecdote: give an example from your experience that engages the student and creates a context for learning

Additional Sections

Use the “Section 2” tool to show a new section before the Course Description. Use the ”Section 4” and ”Section 5” tools to place new sections after the Course Description.
 

Outcomes help

OAR model

The Syllabus Builder uses the Objectives-Resources-Activities (OAR) model as a development framework.

Learn more about the OAR model

Objectives are statements created to define and/or measure desired student behavior. Learning Outcomes are statements that address the student directly, and incorporate an action verb.

The default design of the Learning Outcomes section uses a common "stem" for all outcomes:

You may modify this stem by highlighting and typing over the text.* You may also delete this stem if you would prefer to use different stems which are embedded in each Outcome.

Bloom's Revised Taxonomy

The “Bloom's Revised” tool incorporates six levels of learning: remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate and create. Clicking on a level opens a window with a group of action verb buttons that describe behaviors associated with the level. Clicking on a button inserts the action verb into a text field.

Additional Sections

Use the “Section 2” tool to add a new section after the Learning Outcomes.

OAR model

Learning Management System

View Policies help

Required Software

A detailed list of all software needed for the course. DO NOT assume that students will have commonly used software such as Microsoft Word or Microsoft PowerPoint. You may wish to provide information about open-source alternatives such as OpenOffice Writer.

Visit the Open Office website

If you are team teaching and/or have a Teaching Assistant, use the ”add contact” tool to add up to three additional Contacts.

Textbook and/or other Reading Materials

Information about textbooks, course packets, journal articles, and other reading materials used in the course. Details such as textbook ISDN number, where to purchase reading materials and access information for password-protected digital assets.

Videos

Information about videos and/or other multimedia that will be used in the course. Also information about where (LMS, external website, etc.) and how (login information) students will access these resources. Information on software needed to run multimedia (Flash, HTML 5 browser, etc.) in Required Software.

Activities help

OAR model

The Syllabus Builder uses the Objectives-Resources-Activities (OAR) model as a development framework.

Learn more about the OAR model

Acitivites are opportunities to created to influence and/or measure defined student behavior. Learning Outcomes are combined with Learning Resources to create Learning Activities. There are two types of Learning Activities:
  • Application Activities are opportunities for students to demonstrate knowledge and skills
  • Acquisition Activities are opportunities for students to gain knowledge and skills
Provide general information on each Learning Activity: type, frequency and relationship to other Learning Activities.

Policies help

Suggested Headings

At the minimum, in the Course Policies section of your syllabus provide:

Instructor Feedback/Communication

The types of feedback and communication the instructor will provide, and which communication tools will be used. The response time for each type and tool.

Student Feedback/Communication

The types of feedback and communication the instructor will accept, and which communication tools will be used. The response time for each type and tool.

Syllabus Changes

The actions you will take if the syllabus must be revised. The actions students should take if there is a discrepancy between the syllabus, and other sources of information, i.e. - the learning management system.

Late Work

Your expectations regarding late work:
  • Acceptable circumstances
  • Student responsibilties
  • Penalties

Academic Honesty

A thorough definition of academic dishonesty, and the actions that you will take in response to confirmed violation.

Students with Disabilities

The process by which students with disabilities will receive assistance.

Additional Sections

Use the “Section 2” and “Section 3” tools to add sections that distinguish between policies required by different organizational levels, such as:
  • Institution
  • College
  • Department
  • Program
 

Grades help


Sections

At the minimum, in the Information portion of your syllabus provide:

Components

Components are point-based Learning Activities. In the first column type in the component name and quantity/points per (if needed). In the second column type in the amount of points for the component. “Total Points” are calculated automatically.

Scale

The Scale is calculated automatically based on “Percentages” and “Total Points”.
